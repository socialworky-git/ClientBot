Empathy as a Pathway to Screening

Reflect, summarize the meaning, and move toward a screening question

Work through each scenario together as a group. Respond empathically to the client's pain, summarize the meaning beneath their distress, and use that understanding to introduce a suicide screening question. When you've drafted your responses, ask for feedback.

Scenario 1
Client
I try to keep it together for everyone, but most days I'm just empty. I smile when people talk to me, but inside I feel like I'm breaking. I don't even know who I am anymore.
Your reflection capture the feeling and meaning, not just the words
Client
It's like I'm performing all the time. People say I seem strong, but they have no idea how hard it is to keep up the act. I'm so tired of pretending.
Your reflection
Client
Sometimes I wish someone would notice that I'm not really fine. But then, if they did, I don't even know what I'd say. I don't want to fall apart in front of people.

Now bring it together. Build the move in three parts:

Summary statement gather the threads so the client feels heard
Linking phrase bridge from their pain toward the question
Suicide screening question direct, clear, and caring
