Intentional Reflections

Motivational interviewing practice

Every statement below mixes sustain talk with change talk. Find the change talk and craft a reflection that highlights or deepens it.

How it works:
  1. Read the statement and notice the change talk mixed in with the sustain talk.
  2. Write a reflection that highlights or deepens that change talk. A reflection is a statement, not a question.
  3. Check your reflection to see how it moved the field. Tap “Show change talk” if you get stuck.
Sustain talk has gravity on its side. When someone is ambivalent, the status quo gets a head start, so they tend to drift toward staying the same. An intentional reflection levels the field by giving the change talk weight and a fair chance.
