Countertransference Clues

Reading your reactions to a client, and deciding what they mean

How this works. Strong reactions to clients are normal. Sometimes annoyance is just annoyance, and frustration is just a hard day. But when reactions are intense, repeating, or pulling you away from your usual clinical stance, they can be clues that a personality pattern is playing out in the relationship itself. Choose your assigned client, read the clinician's account, and talk it through as a group. Then use the trait worksheet to describe what seems evident in the presentation.

The clinician's account

Talk it through

  1. What is the clinician feeling, thinking, and wanting to do?
  2. What client traits and behaviors might be evoking these reactions?
  3. Which reactions might point to the client's pattern, and which might just be ordinary frustration or something of the clinician's own?
  4. If this pattern shows up in the client's life outside the room, what does it look like?
