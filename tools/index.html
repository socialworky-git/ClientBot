Practice tools and interactive activities for social work training

Reflection drill
Reflection Batting Practice
A fast, playful game: ten client statements get pitched to you, each with its own change focus, and your reflections are scored like baseball. Simple reflections are singles; reflections that grow change talk are home runs.

Affirmation practice
Mining for Affirmations
Spot a client's real strengths, then turn each one into an affirmation that centers the client rather than your reaction. Optional AI feedback flags what reads as praise and suggests stronger wording.
Open →
Reflection practice
Intentional Reflections
Each statement mixes change talk with sustain talk. Reflect the change talk, then check your work: AI feedback shows whether your reflection leveled the field toward change or left it tilted to the status quo.
Open →
Evocation practice
Balancing the Scale
Practice evocation with one deeply ambivalent client across eight exchanges. Send each response as an intentional reflection or a directional question, and watch the scale tip as you grow change talk and level the field toward change.
